Plain-English glossary

Every term across the models, in normal words.
CAC — Customer Acquisition Cost
What it costs in ads to get one buyer. Spend £150, get 10 buyers, CAC is £15. Lower is better.
LTV — Lifetime Value
What one buyer is worth in total, across everything they ever buy. Higher is better.
The edge / spread
The gap between LTV and CAC (£58 vs £15 = ~3.9x). The engine of the business, the interest rate on your money.
AOV — Average Order Value
Average spend in a single purchase (~£17 front-end). One transaction, not lifetime.
ROAS — Return On Ad Spend
Revenue back per £1 of ads. £1 in, £3 back = 3x. The edge as a ratio.
CPL — Cost Per Lead
Like CAC but for a free opt-in (an email), not a buyer. Want it very low (£1–4).
Pixel
Code on your pages that tells Meta what people did (visited, opted in, bought), so ads can learn and be measured.
Lead / Purchase (events)
Moments the pixel reports. A Lead is an email opt-in; a Purchase is a sale. Meta optimises toward whichever you pick.
Funnel
The path a stranger travels: ad → page → opt-in/buy → upsell → climb the ladder.
Value ladder
The rungs (free → £14 → £39 → membership). Each rung sells the next; that's how LTV climbs.
Lead magnet / tripwire
The lead magnet is the free thing that gets the email. The tripwire is the cheap first paid thing (£14) that turns a lead into a buyer.
Upsell / order bump / OTO
An upsell is a bigger offer after buying (£39 System). An order bump is the checkout tick-box (£9). An OTO is a one-time offer shown right after purchase.
MRR — Monthly Recurring Revenue
The predictable money in every month from the membership. The compounding principal; the part that makes a business sellable.
Churn
The rate members cancel each month. Low churn = the recurring base stacks; high churn = it leaks.
Velocity (first-30-day cash)
How fast a buyer's value comes back, not just how much. Fast cash lets you buy next month's ads sooner, the biggest lever on self-funding.
Reinvest / recycle
Ploughing returned cash back into ads so the machine grows itself.
Float
Money out in the market on ads before the revenue returns. Your cushion-at-risk.
Self-funding / crossover
The month the machine funds its own next ads without your top-up.
Machine output
The cash that comes back each month to recycle. Arrived cash, not profit, not lifetime value.
GHL / CRM
GoHighLevel is your all-in-one: contacts, checkout, emails, automations. Your CRM is the contact database inside it.
Tag
A label on a contact (lead-magnet, buyer-tripwire, buyer-system) tracking where they are on the ladder and which emails they get.
Webhook / CAPI
A webhook instantly pings one system from another (page → GHL "new lead"). CAPI is a more accurate, server-side way to report sales to Meta. (Later.)
The two clocks
Cash-now (money for family/living, urgent) vs leverage (building the owned, compounding asset). Score every move on both.
