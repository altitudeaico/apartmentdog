The insight. Linear income is a straight line: fixed budget, flat buyers, flat growth. Compounding curves upward because each month's surplus buys next month's buyers, and the membership base stacks on top of itself every month (new members minus churn). The recurring base is the only part that keeps paying after you stop spending, and the only part that gives the business a sellable multiple. Push reinvest too high and the float deepens, so size it against the cushion you can actually commit while Conquer holds your life. The edge multiple (LTV ÷ CAC) is the interest rate on the whole machine; everything you do to widen it, lower CAC, deepen the ladder, raise retention, compounds harder.
