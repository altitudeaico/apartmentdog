Time to Self-Funding / when the machine feeds itself

Option 2, drilled in. You seed a small monthly top-up; the machine returns cash; you recycle it. The question is how many months until the machine throws off enough on its own to fund £5k, then £15k, a month without your top-up. The hidden lever is not lifetime value, it's velocity: how much of each buyer's worth lands in the first 30 days. Drag it and watch the crossover move.

Plain-English glossary

Every term on this page, in normal words.
CAC — Customer Acquisition Cost
What it costs in ads to get one buyer. Spend £150 on ads, get 10 buyers, your CAC is £15. You want it low.
LTV — Lifetime Value
What one buyer is worth to you in total, across everything they ever buy (the guide, the bump, the System, months of membership). You want it high.
The edge / the spread
The gap between LTV and CAC. At £15 CAC and £58 LTV your edge is ~3.9x, each buyer is worth nearly four times what they cost. This is the engine of the whole thing.
Velocity (cash back in first 30 days)
How fast a buyer's value comes back, not just how much. Two buyers worth £58 each are very different if one pays most of it now and one dribbles it over months. Fast cash is what lets you buy next month's ads sooner.
Reinvest / recycle
Ploughing returned cash back into more ads instead of banking it, so the machine grows itself. Like a snowball.
Machine output
The cash that comes back to you each month that you can spend on ads again. It's arrived cash, not profit and not lifetime value. When it clears £5k, the machine can fund £5k of ads on its own.
Self-funding / crossover
The month the machine throws off enough on its own to pay for its next ads without your top-up. The whole goal here.
MRR — Monthly Recurring Revenue
The predictable money in every month from the £20 membership. 460 members = £9,200 MRR. The part that compounds and makes the business sellable.
Float
The money you have out in the market on ads at any moment, spent before the revenue comes back. Your cushion-at-risk.
Why velocity beats lifetime value here. Two machines can have the same £58 lifetime value, but the one that returns more of it in the first 30 days reaches self-funding far sooner, because that cash comes back in time to buy next month's buyers. So for Option 2, the fastest lever is not the membership (it drips over months), it's the immediate upsells: the order bump and the System offer that convert in the first 48 hours. Push the velocity dial and watch the crossover months drop. Everything here still rides on the real CAC and take-rates the live campaigns are about to report; drop those in and the crossover becomes a date, not an estimate.
